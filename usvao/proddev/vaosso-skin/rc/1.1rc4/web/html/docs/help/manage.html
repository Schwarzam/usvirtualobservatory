Managing Your VAO Identity

Once you have gotten a VAO Login--also referred to as your VAO Identity--this portal gives you a few tools for managing your identity. Described below in more detail (click on the link), these tools include:

Profile Editing Page
View and update our information about you (email, institution, phone, etc.).
Privacy Preferences Page
Change what information you share with portals about you (email, phone, delegated security credentials, etc.).
Certificate Download Page
Download an X.509 certificate and private key for use with your desktop applications.
Password Management Page
Change your password or request a reset by email.
Perhaps you are having a specific problem with your logon. Try looking up your problem in our FAQ section on "Using and Managing your VAO Logon".

Edit Your User Profile Information

B.4. What if I move to a new home institution or change my email address?

When you register to create a VAO login, we ask you for information about yourself. This is referred to as you VAO Identity Profile. When you log into VAO-compatible portals, the portal can ask for some or all of this information (with your permission). Over time--say, if you move to a new job at a different institution--you may wish to update this information. The Profile Editing Page allows you to do this.

When you access the Profile Editing Page, you will be prompted to log into the VAO if you aren't already logged in. When you do, you will see a profile editing form that is filled out with your current profile information:

To update your information:

  1. Edit any of the values in the form as desired.
  2. Make sure values in the email address boxes match.
  3. Enter your password into the last field (labeled "Current Password")
  4. Click the "Update" button.

If the change is successful, a notice labeled "Updated" will appear above the form. An error will be reported to the screen if

  • the email addresses do not match,
  • the password is not correct, or
  • any of the required fields are empty.
If an error occurs, correct the values in the form accordingly and then hit "Update" again. Note that you cannot update your username.

Set your Privacy Preferences

Related FAQ questions:
B.2. Why am I asked to confirm that I want to log in to a portal?
B.3. Why do you share some of my personal information?

When a portal wants for some information about you as part of the login process, you will be presented with a page to confirm your approval. This page also gives you the option to let the VAO Login Services remember this approval so that you are not asked again in the future. (If in the future the portal asks for different information that you haven't approved, it will asked you again to confirm.) If you click "Remember these settings..." check-box, your approval will be save as your privacy preferences. These can be later viewed and changed via the Privacy Preferences Page.

When you access the Privacy Preferences Page, you will be prompted to log into the VAO if you aren't already logged in. When you do, you will see a table that lists for each portal you have visited your current privacy settings--that is, which profile data you have approved for sharing with that portal:

In the example above, you can see at the left the two portals that the user has visited (the "Acme Archive" and "zinc"). At the top, the user's current profile data is shown. In the intersection, you see where is says "Yes" which pieces of information have been approved for sharing with that portal. The "Secure Credentials" indicates whether you will allow the portal to have a temporary certificate that will give it access to other secure services on your behalf. The last column, "Always confirm sharing", indicates whether the Login service will always display the confirmation page when the portal asks for your information. If you previously checked the "Remember these settings..." box, this column will say "No".

To change these choices, click on the "Edit" link next to the portals name. This will cause the view to change to an editable form:

You can then change your preferences by clicking on (or off) the check-box for the profile information you are willing to share with that portal. You can also make the login service always ask for confirmation of these preferences when you login; do this by clicking the check-box by "Always ask...". To commit your changes, click "Update these choices". You can cancel your changes by clicking "Cancel Choices". Clicking either will return the view to the listing. Any committed changes will be shown.

Which data should I share?
This depends on your trust of the portal. Portals that are recognized by the VAO as compliant portals will have a clear title under the "Portal" column; when you expand that portal's row to edit the preferences, you will also see a short description of the portal. ("Acme Archive" in the example above is a recognized portal.) You should feel comfortable sharing your information with recognized portals because their administrators have agreed to our privacy policy. Unrecognized portals will just appear as a domain name (e.g. "portal.archive.edu"); you should be more careful about what you share with these portals (particularly when it comes to sharing your credentials).

Download an X.509 Certificate

Related FAQ questions:
A.9. What's an X.509 certificate? Do I need one?

In accordance with the intent of the IVOA security standards, we have strived to make our services compatible with tools from the grid community. Some desktop or grid-based tools require that you install an X.509 certificate onto your local computer, into the tool, or into your web browser. If you need a certificate for this purpose, you can obtain one through our Certificate Download Page. Most VAO users, however, will not need this.

When you access the Credential Download Page, you will be prompted to log into the VAO if you aren't already logged in. When you do, will see the form for requesting a certificate:

In order to fill out this form, you need to understand something about what you will get. The credentials will come in two parts: a public certificate and a private key. These will be delivered to you combined into a single package (in one of two possible formats). This package is typically the form that you use to load it into an application that needs it. It is "sealed" with its own package key--that is, a password--that you will set in this form; you will need this key when you load the package into your application.

The certificate you will get will have a temporary lifetime; you can set how long it is valid for. A shorter lifetime is safer: if it is stolen, the thief has a limited time to do anything with it. The disadvantage is that it will need to be replaced sooner if you use the application over an extended period.

To retrieve a certificate, fill out the form:

  1. Choose how long the certificate should be valid for. Choose the shortest time that is convenient for your use.
  2. Enter a package key used to seal the credential package that will get delivered to you.
  3. Choose one of the supported output formats for the package. If you will be loading the certificate into a web browser or Java, pick "PKCS12". If you will be loading the certificate into Globus, choose "PEM".
  4. Enter you login password.
  5. Click the "Download Credential" button.

Your browser should then give you a pop-up for saving the delivered package to your local disk. Note that you should keep this file safe and not accessible by other users.

To load the credential into your application, consult your application's documentation. Loading the certificate into a browser will depend on your browser. As an example, here's how you can load it into Firefox (ref. v12.0):

  1. Select "Preference" from the "Edit" menu.
  2. In the Preferences pop-up window, select the "Advanced" section (from the icons along the top).
  3. Select the "Encryption" tab.
  4. Click the "View Certificates" button to open another pop-up window.
  5. Select the "Your Certificates" tab.
  6. Click on the "Import..." button; this should bring up a file chooser window for selecting your credential package file.
  7. Navigate the filesystem to find and select your PKCS12 credential package file.
  8. The browser may next ask you (via another pop-up) to provide the password to you "Security Storage Device"; this is the password you use to protect your web passwords within your browser.
  9. Another pop-up will ask you for "the password that was used to encrypt this certificate backup". This is asking for your package key.
  10. If you entered the correct key, then the certificate will get loaded. You should now see it listed in the "Your Certificates" tab under a section labeled "Virtual Astronomical Observatory".

Reset Your Password

Related FAQ questions:
B.6. I forgot my password! What can I do?
B.7. I forgot my username! What can I do?
B.8. Can I have more than one login?
B.9. Do you support group accounts? Can I share a login?

Sometimes you need to change your login password. Sometimes you forget your password. Sometimes you might even forget your username. The Password Management Page is here to help. For obvious reasons, you do not need to be logged in to access this page.

Below we discuss:

  • Changing Your Password
  • Get Reminded of Your Username
  • Reset Your Forgotten Password

Changing Your Password

Use the "Change Password" section of the page to change your password. This simple (and familiar) form requires the following inputs:

  • your VAO username,
  • your current password (i.e. what you want to change it from), and
  • your new password (i.e. what you want to change it to). You need to enter the same password twice and these entries must match to protect against typos (since you won't be able to see the characters you type in).

Click the "change" button to commit your change. You will see either a message announcing success ("Password changed") or an error message if there was a problem with your inputs. Possible errors include:

  • the two entries of the new password did not match.
  • the requested password is too weak. This can be because:
    • it is too close to your username
    • it is too short (at least 6 characters are required).
    • it is too close to a banned word (e.g. "password", "123456", etc.)
    • it contains illegal characters.

If you encounter an error, you must re-enter your passwords and click "change" again.

Get Reminded of Your Username

Use the "Username Reminder Email" section of the page if you need to be reminded of your username. The key to this tool is knowing the email you have registered with us. If you have more than one email address and you are not sure which one you used, enter all possible email address into the text box with one address per line. When you click the "Remind" button, we'll check for all of them in our database. For each one that matches, we'll send you an email to that address with all of the usernames associated with that address.

(Yes, it is possible to have more than one VAO account, but we discourage this. Sometimes, though, you just forget that you already have one.)

If you have forgotten your username, then it's likely that you've also forgotten your password. For this reason, the email we send you will include instructions for how to change your password. In particular, it includes a token you will need to change it via the "Reset Your Password" section of the page (see next section).

Reset Your Forgotten Password

If you have forgotten your password you can reset it with the "Reset Your Password" section of the Password Management Page. This process has a few steps that starts with entering your username into the form:

Unless you got one from a username reminder email, you won't have a token to fill in the input box labeled "Secret Token", so you leave that blank and press the "reset" button. With this, we will send you an email (to the address associated with this username) with a token needed to proceed the reset. When you receive the email, you can enter the token string into the second field (or you can click on the link provided in that email).

Once you have resubmitted the form with the token, you will see a new form that will let you set your password:

Enter your new choice for a password twice, and click the "change" button. Note that this update can fail for one of the following reasons:

  • the two entries of the new password did not match.
  • the requested password is too weak. This can be because:
    • it is too close to your username
    • it is too short (at least 6 characters are required).
    • it is too close to a banned word (e.g. "password", "123456", etc.)
    • it contains illegal characters.

If you encounter an error, just enter new passwords and click "change" again.
