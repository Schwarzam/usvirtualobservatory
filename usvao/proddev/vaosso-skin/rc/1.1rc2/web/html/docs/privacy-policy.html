VAO Security and Privacy Policies

The VAO Login Services are provided to make it easier for scientists, educators, and students to access and share proprietary data throughout the Virtual Observatory environment. It allows for the establishment of a platform for research that spans across archives and web sites that are managed by different institutions.

This document describes the VAO Login Services terms of use, security policies, and privacy policies. These apply to both sites that support VAO Logins and users that establish VAO identities to use those sites.

In summary, the key points from this document to keep in mind:

  • Use of the VAO Login services are for legitimate scientific and educational purposes.
  • The VAO may block users or web sites from using the VAO Login services that violate our terms of use.
  • To support a federated identity, the VAO services will share your registration data with third parties but only after securing explicit, "opt-in" permission from you.
  • We employ best practices for protecting your information, password, and other data from unauthorized access; however, we recognize that in the IT world there is no absolute guarantee of security. Consequently, this protection is provided on a best effort basis.
You should take these points into consideration when entrusting data into this system.

User Data and Responsible Parties

Data Held on VAO-managed Systems

An aim of the VAO Login services is to provide secure, federated identities across separately administered web sites and services. When a user participates in the VAO Login framework, some data and personal information are held by VAO-administered sites, and some by partner sites which are not under VAO control. Users should familiarize themselves with the policies of both VAO and non-VAO sites that they visit.

The VAO Login Services collects and maintains (or may do so in future) the following information about users:

  • personal information requested at registration time (when a user creates their VAO identity). This information is shared with collaborating web sites upon permission by the user (though the OpenID protocol).
  • group membership information established by users explicitly for sharing data with other users.
  • other affiliation information that helps verify the true identity of the user and collected with the explicit cooperation of the user.
  • logs of accesses to its services by users to help users troubleshoot problems and to assess the effectiveness of our services.

The VAO project provides other services and tools that allow user to save data associated with their VAO identity; we refer to such data items as user data products.. The user can later retrieve their user data products and/or share them securely with other users or web sites with the help of VAO Login services.

Data Held on Third-party Systems

A non-VAO web site, which we refer to as a third-party portal, can leverage VAO Login services to authenticate users that visit the site; this is done through the OpenID protocol. Such a site can then store data products associated with the user's VAO identity. A third-party portal may wish to collect user information as part of a registration process. (Some of this may be collected by requesting it from the VAO via the OpenID protocol, requiring the user's permission, or by asking the user for it directly.) The portal may also collect logging information recording accesses to the site by the user.

While the VAO is not responsible for the handling of data and information held by third-party sites, all sites that use VAO logins are subject to the terms of use spelled out in this document. The VAO maintains records of sites that have explicitly agreed to these terms and are thereby "known" to the VAO; however, sites may make use of the open VAO login services without prior notification. Sites that violate the terms of use may be blocked from using VAO services.

User Responsibilities

A user with a VAO login account (i.e. a VAO identity) and who uses services protected by VAO authentication may create or download data products in the normal course of using those services. The VAO is not responsible for products stored on the user's own sytems. User data products stored on portal web sites should not be considered safe from loss due to system failure or human error unless the site explicitly documents such otherwise; users are responsible for backing up their own data to protect against loss.

The VAO framework for federated identities is optimized for one login account per user. Multiple accounts for a single user are discouraged but not disallowed. Group accounts in which a single password is shared amoung multiple users are highly discouraged.

The VAO asks users that when they create a VAO identity that they accept the following responsibilites:

  • abide by the terms described in the "Restrictions on Use" below,
  • take reasonable measures to protect their password from discovery by others and not to share their password with others,
  • practice good "Netizenship" and conduct themselves professionally and courteously while logged into VAO and VAO-compatible portal.

Caveat Emptor

The VAO Login services available via the site at https://sso.usvao.org/ are provided by the VAO project for research and educational purposes only. They should not be used to support commercial or other monetary transactions. While we strive to ensure a high degree of security, data integrity, and user support, these services are provided "as-is" and on a best-effort basis, without any implied guarantees. Users are advised against using these services for purposes in which data loss or a unauthorized disclosure would result in significant monetary loss or physical damage. The VAO, LLC shall not be held liable for loss or unauthorized disclosure of user data or information.

Restrictions of Use

The VAO login and data services must not be used for any of the follow purposes:

  • to directly support commercial or other monetary transactions,
  • to support activities of any kind in violation of U.S. law, including the unlawful distribution of copyrighted or illicit materials,
  • to attempt to circumvent the authentication and authorization mechanisms of any VAO portal or service or of any VAO-compatible portal or service,
  • to promote a hostile environment for other users, and
  • to support uses other than those that advance scientific research or education unless otherwise approved by the VAO project.

When an application wishes to charge a user for a service (e.g. a conference registration fee), any collection of personal financial information, including credit card numbers, must be done outside the security realm protected by VAO Login services. Sites supporting VAO Login services must not use VAO Login services to authenticate users to authorize access to any such financial information. The VAO, LLC accepts no responsibility loss or disclosure of financial information.

Web sites supporting VAO Logins must not attempt to capture or cache a user's password in either encrypted or unencrypted form without express permission to do so by VAO Login Services administrators; user authentication must be handled only by approved VAO services operated by the VAO and approved VAO partners. Web sites must not serve web pages made to look like VAO web pages without express permission.

The VAO reserves the right to block any user or web site that violate these restrictions from using VAO services.

Controlling Access to Your Information and Data

Sharing Registration Information with Permission

When you, as a user, register to create a VAO identity, you provide the VAO with some personal information including an email address, country of residence, etc. When you authenticate with a web site (i.e. log into it) using a VAO login name, the web site may request access to any of this information. VAO Login services will share this registration information only after you provide explicit permission to do so.. This is done by presenting a page that lists the information being requested and asks for your permission to share it. You can choose to have the VAO remember this decision so you are not asked again. The VAO Login services allow you later to change this decision at any time later. (See your the privacy prefences page for details.)

To help advise users when sharing information, the VAO keeps records of known collaborating web sites who agreed explicitly to abide by the VAO's terms of use; VAO services will note when such a web site is so recognized.

Sharing Data Products Across Sites

As a result of the VAO's system of federated identities, a user can have data associated with their VAO identity at many different VAO and third-party portals. The VAO Login services can enable one portal to access a user's products at another portal on the user's behalf. To accomplish this, a portal can request an X.509 certificate, a digital data item that represents the user's identity. The VAO will provide the portal with the certificate only after receiving permission from the user. (The user can have the VAO Login services remember this choice to provide certificates to the portal without further permission.) Other portals may choose to accept an X.509 certificate as authentication of the user; thus, the portal holding the certificate can present the certificate to services at other portals and access the user's data.

Access via X.509 certificates issued by the VAO Login services have two important limitations:

  • The certificates are valid for a limited time. This time may by set by the user. The maximum validity time is limited by VAO policy.
  • Depending on the policy of the portal accepting the certificate as authentication, the certificate may enable access to all data products belonging to the user. The certificate does not specify internally any limitation on what data can be accessed or what other specific authorizations should be granted.

The VAO Login services may in the future provide mechanisms that restrict access to data and services on a finer grain level than described above.

To help advise users on when to allow a web site to access their data on other sites via an X.509 certificates, the VAO maintains records of "known" collaborating portals who have explicitly agreed to the VAO policies and terms of service. Users will be informed when a known portal is requesting an X.509 certificate. The portal should document their services that access user data products on other sites to inform the user what data will be access and how it will be used. Portals should should not access or cache data for purposes other than those documented for the user.

Other Information Sharing

The VAO will not share personal information including email addresses with third parties for any reason outside of the process described above. The VAO will not use users' personal information for distributing email for any purposes other than the maintenance of users' identities. The VAO does not condone the use of user email addresses by third parties that have retrieved them through the legitimate means described above for purposes other that the administration of participating portals. Abuse of user trust and the intent of the VAO Login federation with regard to email address use (such as to deliver spam) will be considered grounds for discontinuing support for a third-party portal.

The VAO will not share any information revealing patterns of use that is traceable to an individual user with third parties with the following exception: administrators of known web sites supporting VAO logins that have agreed to the terms of use may request through a secure service access to information logging access (and access attempts) to their own web sites through the VAO Login services. This is provided to aid in their assisting their users with login troubles. Web site administrators will not have access to logging information related to other web sites.

The VAO collects logging information to compile aggregate statistics to understand the true usage of VAO services. While some user tracking may be done internally to identify related operations, this is done without regard to the specific identity of the users. Aggregate statistics that do not identify individual identities may be published publically.

The VAO strives with best effort to protect each user's personal information, password, and records of usage from access by unauthorized parties. As part of this effort, we apply best practices recognized by industry and IT security community.
