Tips for Creating Safe Passwords

Even if you feel that what your protecting is not very valuable, it is worth being careful when choosing a password both to avoid the pain of dealing with a stolen account and to protect your other on-line identities.

Discussed on this page:
  • Password Don'ts
  • Password Dos
  • Creation Algorithms
  • VAO Restrictions
  • What others recommend

Password Don'ts

  • Don't use the same password that you use for your banking or social media accounts.
  • Don't use a single word from a dictionary. If you use real words as the basis of your password, you should:
    • use multiple, random words, or
    • heavily corrupt the word with extra characters and symbol/number substitutions.
  • Don't use simple keyword patterns on the keyboard like consecutive, adjacent characters on the keyboard.
  • Don't base your password on your username, real name, email address, birthday or other information that can be easily discovered about you.

Password Dos

  • Include different types of characters in your password, including capitals, numbers, symbols, and punctuation.
  • Use sound-alike substitutions in real words.
  • Incorporate made-up words.
  • Base your password on a mneumonic that is not part of your recorded or on-line history.
  • Create an acronym from an easy to remember phrase.
  • Use a desktop password storage application. By storing your passwords in a secure (encrypted and master password-protected) password storage application, you will be less likely to reuse passwords or to pick a weak password because it's easy to remember.

Algorithms for Choosing Strong Passwords

Here are some techniques that some use to create a strong password that can be easy remember.

Base your password on an acronym.

  1. Choose a personal, easy-to-remember phrase, like "The Cubs broke my heart in 08".
  2. Convert this to an acronym: "tcbmhi08".
  3. Apply substitutions and additions: "tCbmhIn08!hc!"

Use a Password Generator

  1. Visit the Simple Strong Password Generator site which will create a long password based on four randomly selected words: "swimbeautifulcheckedcolumn".
  2. (Optional) Apply substitutions and additions: "swim,butifulcheckedcolm"

VAO Restrictions on Passwords

When you create or update your password, we will check the password to see if it is too weak for our standards. Currently, we disallow passwords that:

  • shorter than six characters (there is no maximum limit),
  • are too close to your name, username, email address, or home institution,
  • closely match disallowed words (like "password" or "passw0rd"), or
  • closely match disallowed keyboard sequences (like "asdfgh" or "123456").
