Choosing a Good Password for Your NVO Logon

A good password is one that is unlikely to be guessed by another person or, more importantly, by password-cracking program. To help prevent someone else from logging on the Virtual Observatory with your identity, NVO recommends that you choose a password that meets the following criteria:

At least 6 characters long
Contains a mix of character types
Consider including at least one character from each of the following:
  • Uppercase letters (ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ)
  • Lowercase letters (abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz)
  • Numbers (0123456789)
May also include these other characters non-alpha numeric characters: ,.!?-_()/
No names or name fragments
In particular, do not include fragments from your login name
No large words
If a word helps you remember your password, consider using a word fragment or an altered spelling of the word.
No numeric or other keyboard sequences
Avoid the use of characters that appear in sequence together on the keyboard, such as asd or 987.
Does not match an example password

These recommendations were adapted from password requirements set by the Campus Information Technologies and Educational Services (CITES) at the University of Illinois.
